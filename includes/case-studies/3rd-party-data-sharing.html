Privacy & Data interface

Case Study

Designed status states and notifications so account owners and authorized proxies could review, update, and audit third-party data-sharing permissions.

Overview

I designed a Florida Blue account experience that allowed members to review which third-party apps had access to their protected health information and take action from a single page. Although my title was Visual Designer and had guidance from the interaction team, this project required me to own both the interface design and the interaction decisions because the requirements were highly regulated, still evolving, and often too vague to hand off cleanly. I had to balance scanability, trust, legal compliance, and design-system constraints without introducing new components.

  • Designed a one-page experience to scan app status and manage sharing
  • Owned interaction design decisions alongside visual design execution
  • Worked within strict legal, content, and system constraints
  • Limited to existing components and patterns

Project Summary

Role:
Visual + Interaction Design
Platform:
Responsive Web + Mobile App

3rd-Party Data Sharing

Legal compliance with limited budget

Context & Challenge

Members needed a clear way to see which third-party apps were using their protected health information, understand each app’s status, and stop sharing when needed. The challenge was that the page also had to include a large amount of required legal copy above the table, all actions had to live on a single page, and there was no budget to create new components or patterns. I had to simplify a dense, compliance-heavy experience using only the existing design system while still making the page easy to scan and act on.

Design Constraints

  • Used existing components only with no budget for new patterns.
  • Included a large amount of required legal and explanatory copy above the table.
  • Designed the page so users could scan, review, and modify all app-sharing relationships in one place.
Result

Turned vague, regulated requirements into a clearer account-management experience that supported trust, faster scanning, and direct action without expanding the system.

Reflection

This project reflects how I often worked at Florida Blue: my title centered on visual design, but the work regularly extended into interaction design, pattern validation, and translating unclear business requirements into usable interfaces. On this effort, I had to make disciplined trade-offs, using hierarchy, spacing, status indicators, and content structure to reduce friction without removing required information. It pushed me to design with precision inside real-world constraints instead of relying on ideal conditions.

“Strong visual design is not decoration. In regulated product spaces, it becomes the structure that helps users understand risk, trust the system, and take action with confidence.”
