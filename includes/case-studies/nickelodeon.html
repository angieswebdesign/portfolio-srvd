Nickelodeon Interactive TV App

Case Study

Overview

I designed the interface for a web-based TV application deployed across Nickelodeon Hotels & Resorts Punta Cana guest suites. The experience was built for large-format, distance-based viewing and remote-control navigation. Design decisions prioritized legibility at a distance, simplified interaction patterns, and strict adherence to Nickelodeon brand standards to ensure consistency across digital and physical guest touchpoints.

The result was a scalable, branded TV platform adaptable across properties, devices, and future feature expansion.

Research

Methods: Competitive Analysis, User Personas, Brand & Environmental Research

I begin each project by grounding the work in the client’s brand, environment, and audience context. For hospitality and in-room TV experiences, this includes evaluating how visual language, spatial cues, and interaction patterns translate to large-format interfaces.

Research focused on existing brand systems, tone of voice, photography style, and competitive hospitality experiences, as well as architectural and interior design cues that could inform layout and form. Accessibility considerations such as distance legibility, contrast, and clarity were also assessed.

Architectural cues—such as dominant curves—were translated into repeatable UI elements to reinforce brand identity and visual distinction.

These insights informed early layout decisions, interaction models, and visual motifs while ensuring alignment with both brand expectations and technical constraints.

High-Fidelity Mockups

Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator

Research findings were translated into high-fidelity UI mockups aligned with brand guidelines and platform constraints. Initial designs focused on the home screen and key inner modules to establish visual direction and secure client approval.

My front-end knowledge supported closer collaboration with engineering during this phase, helping prevent unnecessary design iterations and late-stage rework.

Following sign-off, additional screens were designed and iterated through multiple feedback rounds, balancing client requests with feasibility and timeline considerations. Annotated mockups were used to document changes and guide implementation.

User Interface (UI) Design

Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator, HTML, CSS, Content Management

I served as a Senior UI Designer at project kickoff and transitioned into the Lead UI Designer role during later phases, creating and exporting native graphic assets using annotated wireframes and mockups as a guide. Assets followed strict file naming and folder conventions required by the development team, while designs adhered to Apple Human Interface Guidelines and Android Material Design standards across supported devices. Where appropriate, interactive TV graphics were created in SVG format, and front-end styling was applied using HTML and CSS to support accurate implementation.

Internal Team Meetings

Including Account Managers, Designers, Developers, Database Administrators, Quality Assurance Testers

Throughout the project lifecycle, I collaborated closely with account managers, designers, developers, database administrators, and QA testers through regular SCRUM meetings to identify blockers, address cross-team dependencies, and align on scope or timeline changes. As a senior designer, I also supported other designers with tooling and environment issues and assisted with design problem-solving as needed to keep work moving forward.

Application and QA Testing

Xcode, Android Studio, Virtual Devices & Native Hardware (Mobile, Tablet, TV), GitHub, HTML/CSS, Microsoft SQL Server, Jira

Once required graphic assets were complete, I tested the application across virtual and native environments to validate stability, performance, and UI integrity. Testing initially focused on virtual devices, with native hardware validation occurring later due to limited device availability.

To simulate real-world usage, I seeded and validated test users directly in the database and verified application builds against the latest repository commits to ensure design–code parity and prevent regressions.

When issues occurred, I diagnosed crashes and rendering failures by isolating root causes across design assets, application logic, platform updates, and database dependencies. IDE stack traces were analyzed to identify failure points, and findings were documented with annotated screenshots.

I audited database schemas and field mappings to confirm data integrity and correct system behavior. Unresolved issues were documented and prioritized in Jira with clear reproduction steps and visual evidence, then escalated to platform-specific engineers with precise technical context to reduce investigation time.

Graphic Asset Handoff

Tools: Zip files, Microsoft SQL Server

After testing and cross-device validation, graphic assets were prepared for handoff to the development and database teams using standardized file structures and versioning. Test data was cleared from the database, environments were prepared for client use, and project status was finalized in Jira with supporting documentation for future reference.

App Publishing

Destinations: App Store, Google Play

Final application packaging and release workflows were completed in collaboration with engineering and database teams to support public deployment through the App Store and Google Play.

Summary

This project enabled the client to deploy and iterate branded, device-specific applications across multiple sub-brands under tight timelines. The modular framework supported future feature expansion while maintaining usability, performance, and visual consistency across platforms.
